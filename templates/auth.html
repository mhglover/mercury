What is this?

Mercury Radio is a live playlist agent. As you listen to Spotify, it sends recommendations to your player so that you get a mix of music that you, specifically you, like.

Ratings

Mercury keeps a ratings database where it tracks how you feel about each song that plays. When you log in, it checks your recently played history for songs that you like and your Saved Songs for ones that you love. As you listen to the Radio, it adds ratings for every recommendation you hear.

Recommendations

Mercury combines the ratings and histories of active users to pick songs that y'all already like, love, or have never rated. It tries to avoid tracks that any of you have heard recently. It automatically sends tracks to your player queue about 30 seconds before your current track ends.

This Sucks, Change It

If you get a Recommendation you don't like, hit Play Next in your Spotify player and it will play the next track in your local playlist. Mercury will notice the early track change and add a negative rating for you. When that track ends, you'll get a new recommendation that will, hopefully, be a banger.

Spotify Permissions

Mercury will look at your Spotify saved songs and player history to derive ratings. While you are active on the Mercury app and listening to Spotify, it will monitor your Spotify player status every 1-30 seconds.

Privacy Policy

Mercury Radio will never share any of your data or any data about you. To delete your Mercury Radio account and all data associated with your account, use the Nuke My Account option on your account page.
